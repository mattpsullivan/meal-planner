## Component Recipes

### Grains

#### Jasmine Rice (Big Batch)

**Yield:** ~8 cups | **Storage:** 5 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 3 cups jasmine rice, rinsed
- 4.5 cups water
- 1 tsp kosher salt

**Instructions:**
1. Combine rice, water, and salt in pot or rice cooker.
2. Bring to boil, reduce to low, cover, cook 15-18 minutes.
3. Fluff and let steam 5 minutes.
4. Divide: 4 cups for curry/meals, 2 cups for fried rice, 2 cups for pudding.

---

### Proteins

#### Thai Green Curry

**Yield:** ~8 cups | **Storage:** 5 days refrigerated, freezes well

**Ingredients:**
- 2 tbsp coconut oil
- 4 tbsp Thai green curry paste (vegan brand)
- 2 cans (13.5 oz each) full-fat coconut milk
- 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut cream
- 1 cup vegetable broth
- 1 medium eggplant, cubed
- 2 zucchini, sliced into half-moons
- 1 bell pepper, sliced
- 3-4 baby bok choy, quartered
- 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained (optional for protein)
- 2 tbsp coconut aminos
- 1 tbsp coconut sugar
- 1 tbsp lime juice
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- Fresh Thai basil leaves

**Instructions:**
1. Heat coconut oil in large pot over medium. Add curry paste, fry 2 minutes until fragrant.
2. Add coconut milk and cream. Stir well to combine with paste.
3. Add broth. Bring to simmer.
4. Add eggplant first (needs most time). Simmer 5 minutes.
5. Add zucchini and bell pepper. Simmer 5 minutes.
6. Add bok choy and chickpeas. Simmer 3-4 minutes until bok choy is tender.
7. Stir in coconut aminos, sugar, lime juice, and salt.
8. Remove from heat, stir in basil.
9. Taste and adjust seasoning.

#### Lentil Bolognese

**Yield:** ~7 cups | **Storage:** 5 days refrigerated, 3 months frozen

**Ingredients:**
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 large yellow onion, finely diced
- 2 carrots, finely diced
- 2 celery stalks, finely diced
- 6 cloves garlic, minced
- 1.5 cups green or brown lentils, rinsed
- 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
- 3 tbsp tomato paste
- 1/2 cup red wine or vegetable broth
- 2 cups vegetable broth
- 2 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp dried basil
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1/4 tsp red pepper flakes
- Fresh basil for garnish

**Instructions:**
1. Heat oil in large pot over medium. Add onion, carrots, celery. Cook 8-10 minutes until soft.
2. Add garlic, cook 1 minute.
3. Add lentils, tomatoes, tomato paste, wine, broth, herbs, and seasonings.
4. Bring to boil, reduce to low simmer. Cover and cook 35-45 minutes until lentils are tender.
5. Remove bay leaves. Taste and adjust.
6. Sauce should be thick. If too thin, simmer uncovered a few more minutes.

#### White Beans with Herbs

**Yield:** ~4 cups | **Storage:** 5 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 4 cloves garlic, sliced thin
- 1 sprig fresh rosemary
- 4-5 sprigs fresh thyme
- 2 cans (15 oz each) cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
- 1/2 cup vegetable broth
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- 1/4 tsp red pepper flakes
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- Fresh parsley for garnish

**Instructions:**
1. Heat oil in skillet over medium-low. Add garlic, cook gently until golden, 2-3 minutes (don't burn!).
2. Add rosemary and thyme. Let sizzle 30 seconds.
3. Add beans, broth, salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes.
4. Increase heat, simmer 8-10 minutes until liquid reduces and beans are creamy.
5. Remove herb stems. Stir in lemon juice.
6. Mash some beans slightly for creamier texture.

**Use in:** Breakfast (savory), pasta topping, grain bowls, on toast

#### Crispy Chickpea "Larb"

**Yield:** ~3 cups | **Storage:** 4 days refrigerated

Larb is a Thai salad - this version uses chickpeas instead of meat.

**Ingredients:**
- 2 tbsp coconut oil
- 2 cans (15 oz each) chickpeas, drained, rinsed, and dried
- 4 shallots, thinly sliced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 inch ginger, minced
- 2-3 Thai chilies, minced (adjust to taste)
- 3 tbsp coconut aminos
- 2 tbsp lime juice
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tsp coconut sugar
- 1/2 tsp ground cumin
- 1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped
- 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
- 3 green onions, sliced
- 2 tbsp roasted peanuts, crushed

**Instructions:**
1. Heat oil in large skillet over medium-high. Add chickpeas, spread in single layer.
2. Cook without stirring 3-4 minutes until bottoms crisp. Stir, repeat.
3. Push chickpeas aside, add shallots. Cook 3 minutes until soft.
4. Add garlic, ginger, and chilies. Cook 1 minute.
5. Add coconut aminos, lime juice, vinegar, sugar, and cumin. Toss everything together.
6. Remove from heat. Stir in herbs and green onions.
7. Top with crushed peanuts.
8. Store base (without fresh herbs). Add fresh herbs and peanuts when serving.

### Sauces & Dressings

#### Peanut Lime Dressing

**Yield:** ~1.5 cups | **Storage:** 10 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 1/2 cup natural peanut butter
- 3 tbsp coconut aminos
- 3 tbsp lime juice
- 2 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp maple syrup
- 1 inch ginger, grated
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- 1/4-1/2 cup warm water

**Instructions:**
1. Whisk all ingredients except water.
2. Add water gradually to reach pourable consistency.
3. Store in jar. Shake before using.

---

#### Creamy Cashew Alfredo

**Yield:** ~3 cups | **Storage:** 5 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup raw cashews, soaked 15 min in boiling water, drained
- 1 can (13.5 oz) full-fat coconut milk
- 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
- 3 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1/4 tsp nutmeg
- Fresh basil or parsley for garnish

**Instructions:**
1. Blend cashews, coconut milk, nutritional yeast, lemon juice, garlic, salt, pepper, and nutmeg until completely smooth.
2. Transfer to saucepan. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until thickened, 5-7 minutes.
3. Taste and adjust seasoning.
4. Thin with more coconut milk if needed.

---

#### Quick Marinara

**Yield:** ~3 cups | **Storage:** 7 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1/2 tsp dried basil
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- Pinch of sugar
- Fresh basil for garnish

**Instructions:**
1. Heat oil in saucepan over medium. Add garlic, cook 1 minute.
2. Add tomatoes, herbs, salt, pepper, and sugar.
3. Simmer 15-20 minutes until thickened.
4. Use immersion blender for smoother texture (optional).

### Roasted Vegetables

#### Thai-Spiced Sweet Potatoes

**Yield:** ~4 cups | **Storage:** 5 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 3 medium sweet potatoes, cubed
- 2 tbsp coconut oil, melted
- 1 tbsp coconut aminos
- 1 tsp curry powder
- 1/2 tsp ground coriander
- 1/4 tsp cayenne
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- Lime wedges and cilantro for serving

**Instructions:**
1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
2. Toss sweet potatoes with oil, coconut aminos, and spices.
3. Spread on sheet pan.
4. Roast 25-30 minutes, stirring halfway, until tender and caramelized.

---

#### Italian Roasted Vegetables

**Yield:** ~5 cups | **Storage:** 5 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 2 zucchini, cut into half-moons
- 2 bell peppers, cut into chunks
- 2 cups cherry tomatoes
- 1 small red onion, cut into wedges
- 4 tbsp olive oil
- 4 cloves garlic, smashed
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1/2 tsp dried basil
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- Fresh basil for garnish

**Instructions:**
1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
2. Toss vegetables with oil, garlic, herbs, salt, and pepper.
3. Spread on sheet pans.
4. Roast 25-30 minutes until tender and slightly charred.
5. Garnish with fresh basil.

### Breakfast Components

#### Coconut Rice Pudding

**Yield:** 4 servings | **Storage:** 5 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 2 cups cooked jasmine rice
- 1 can (13.5 oz) full-fat coconut milk
- 2 tbsp maple syrup
- 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
- 1/4 tsp cinnamon
- Pinch of cardamom (optional)
- Pinch of salt
- Toppings: sliced banana, mango, toasted coconut, crushed nuts, dried cranberries

**Instructions:**
1. Combine rice, coconut milk, maple syrup, vanilla, cinnamon, cardamom, and salt in saucepan.
2. Cook over medium heat, stirring often, until creamy and thickened, 10-12 minutes.
3. Divide into portions.
4. Serve warm or cold with toppings.

#### Marinated White Beans

**Yield:** ~2 cups | **Storage:** 7 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 1 can (15 oz) cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp lemon juice
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1/4 tsp red pepper flakes
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- Fresh parsley, chopped

**Instructions:**
1. Combine all ingredients in container.
2. Let marinate at least 1 hour (better overnight).
3. Eat as snack, on toast, or in salads.

---

#### Rosemary Roasted Nuts

**Yield:** 1.5 cups | **Storage:** 2 weeks at room temperature

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup raw cashews
- 1/2 cup raw almonds
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, finely chopped
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- Pinch of cayenne

**Instructions:**
1. Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C).
2. Toss nuts with oil, rosemary, salt, pepper, and cayenne.
3. Spread on lined sheet pan.
4. Roast 12-15 minutes, stirring halfway, until golden and fragrant.
5. Cool completely before storing.
