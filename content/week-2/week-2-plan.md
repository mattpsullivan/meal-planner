## Component Recipes

### Grains

#### Coconut Basmati Rice

**Yield:** ~6 cups | **Storage:** 5 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 2 cups basmati rice, rinsed well
- 1 can (13.5 oz) full-fat coconut milk
- 1 cup water
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp ground turmeric (optional, for color)

**Instructions:**
1. Combine rinsed rice, coconut milk, water, salt, and turmeric in pot.
2. Bring to boil, reduce to low, cover, cook 15-18 minutes.
3. Remove from heat, keep covered, rest 5 minutes.
4. Fluff with fork. Cool and store.

**Reheating:** Microwave with splash of water, covered.

---

#### Garlic Noodles

**Yield:** ~6 servings | **Storage:** 4 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 14 oz rice noodles (pad thai style)
- 3 tbsp sesame oil
- 6 cloves garlic, minced
- 3 tbsp coconut aminos
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tsp maple syrup
- 1/4 tsp red pepper flakes
- 3 green onions, sliced
- 2 tbsp sesame seeds

**Instructions:**
1. Cook noodles according to package (usually soak in hot water). Drain and rinse with cold water.
2. Heat sesame oil in large skillet over medium. Add garlic, cook 1 minute until fragrant (don't brown).
3. Add coconut aminos, rice vinegar, maple syrup, and red pepper flakes. Stir.
4. Add noodles, toss to coat evenly.
5. Remove from heat. Toss with green onions and sesame seeds.
6. Cool and store. These are great cold or reheated.

### Proteins

#### Coconut Chickpea Curry

**Yield:** ~6 cups | **Storage:** 5 days refrigerated (freezes well)

**Ingredients:**
- 2 tbsp coconut oil or neutral oil
- 1 large yellow onion, diced
- 6 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 inches fresh ginger, minced
- 1-2 serrano chilies, minced (adjust to heat)
- 2 tbsp curry powder
- 1 tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1/2 tsp ground coriander
- 1 can (13.5 oz) full-fat coconut milk
- 1 can (13.5 oz) crushed tomatoes or diced tomatoes
- 2 cans (15 oz each) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 cup vegetable broth
- Juice of 1 lime
- Fresh cilantro for garnish

**Instructions:**
1. Heat oil in large pot over medium. Add onion, cook 5 minutes until soft.
2. Add garlic, ginger, and chilies. Cook 2 minutes.
3. Add all dry spices. Stir constantly for 1 minute until fragrant.
4. Add coconut milk, tomatoes, chickpeas, salt, and broth. Stir well.
5. Bring to simmer. Cook 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until thickened.
6. Stir in lime juice. Taste and adjust salt/spice.
7. Cool and store. Garnish with cilantro when serving.

#### Peanut Lentils

**Yield:** ~4 cups | **Storage:** 5 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup green or brown lentils
- 2.5 cups water
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1 cup Peanut Sauce (recipe below)

**Instructions:**
1. Rinse lentils. Combine with water and salt in pot.
2. Bring to boil, reduce to simmer, cook 20-25 minutes until tender but not mushy.
3. Drain any excess water.
4. While warm, stir in 1 cup peanut sauce. Mix well.
5. Cool and store. Reserve remaining peanut sauce for other uses.

**Use in:** Noodle bowls, lettuce wraps, Buddha bowls

---

#### Crispy Sesame Cauliflower

**Yield:** ~6 cups | **Storage:** 3 days refrigerated (best eaten within 2 days)

**Ingredients:**
- 2 large heads cauliflower, cut into bite-size florets
- 3 tbsp sesame oil
- 3 tbsp cornstarch or arrowroot
- 1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 tsp black pepper

**For the glaze:**
- 3 tbsp coconut aminos
- 2 tbsp maple syrup
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp sriracha
- 2 tbsp sesame seeds

**Instructions:**
1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Line 2 sheet pans with parchment.
2. Toss cauliflower with sesame oil.
3. In small bowl, mix cornstarch, panko, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
4. Toss cauliflower with coating mixture.
5. Spread in single layer on sheet pans.
6. Bake 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway, until golden and crispy.
7. While baking, whisk glaze ingredients (except sesame seeds) in small bowl.
8. When cauliflower is done, immediately toss with glaze and sesame seeds.
9. Store in container. Reheat in oven or air fryer to re-crisp.

**Note:** For crispiest results, store glaze separately and toss just before serving.

### Sauces & Dressings

#### Peanut Sauce

**Yield:** ~2 cups | **Storage:** 10 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 3/4 cup natural creamy peanut butter
- 1/4 cup coconut aminos
- 3 tbsp rice vinegar
- 2 tbsp maple syrup
- 2 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 inch fresh ginger, grated
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- Juice of 2 limes
- 1/4-1/2 cup warm water (to thin)
- 1/4 tsp cayenne or 1 tsp sriracha (optional)

**Instructions:**
1. Whisk all ingredients except water in bowl.
2. Add warm water gradually until desired consistency (pourable but thick).
3. Taste and adjust lime/sweetness/salt.
4. Store in jar. Shake before using, thin with water if needed.

---

#### Tikka-Style Sauce

**Yield:** ~2.5 cups | **Storage:** 7 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 2 tbsp coconut oil
- 1 medium onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 inch fresh ginger, minced
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 tbsp garam masala
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1/2 tsp ground coriander
- 1/4 tsp cayenne
- 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut cream
- 1/2 cup vegetable broth
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1 tbsp lemon juice

**Instructions:**
1. Heat oil in saucepan over medium. Cook onion 5 minutes until soft.
2. Add garlic and ginger, cook 2 minutes.
3. Add tomato paste and all spices. Stir 1 minute.
4. Add coconut cream and broth. Simmer 10 minutes.
5. Blend with immersion blender (or transfer to blender) until smooth.
6. Stir in lemon juice and salt.
7. Cool and store.

**Use in:** Pour over chickpeas, roasted vegetables, baked potatoes. Also great with the crispy cauliflower.

---

#### Ginger-Sesame Dressing

**Yield:** ~1 cup | **Storage:** 10 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 3 tbsp sesame oil
- 3 tbsp rice vinegar
- 2 tbsp coconut aminos
- 1 tbsp maple syrup
- 1 inch fresh ginger, finely grated
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- 1 tsp sriracha (optional)
- 1 tbsp sesame seeds

**Instructions:**
1. Whisk all ingredients together.
2. Store in jar. Shake before using.

### Roasted Vegetables

#### Curry-Roasted Vegetables

**Yield:** ~6 cups | **Storage:** 5 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 4 carrots, cut into coins
- 2 bell peppers, cut into chunks
- 1 large onion, cut into wedges
- 2 cups cauliflower florets (if you have extra)
- 3 tbsp coconut oil, melted
- 2 tsp curry powder
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1/2 tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper

**Instructions:**
1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
2. Toss all vegetables with oil and spices.
3. Spread on sheet pans in single layer.
4. Roast 30-35 minutes, stirring halfway, until tender and caramelized.
5. Cool and store.

---

#### Stir-Fry Vegetable Mix (Raw Prep)

**Yield:** ~8 cups raw | **Storage:** 5 days refrigerated

**Prep:**
- 2 heads broccoli, cut into florets
- 12 oz snap peas or snow peas, trimmed
- 2 carrots, sliced thin on diagonal
- 2 bunches bok choy, chopped
- 4 green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces

**Instructions:**
1. Prep all vegetables and store together in large container or bag.
2. When ready to stir-fry, heat 2 tbsp sesame oil in wok or large skillet over HIGH heat.
3. Add vegetables in batches, starting with carrots (need most time).
4. Stir-fry 5-7 minutes until crisp-tender.
5. Season with coconut aminos, garlic, ginger, sesame seeds.

### Breakfast Components

#### Mango Chia Pudding (Makes 3)

**Storage:** 5 days refrigerated

**Per jar:**
- 3 tbsp chia seeds
- 3/4 cup full-fat coconut milk
- 1/2 ripe mango, diced (or 1/2 cup frozen, thawed)
- 1 tbsp maple syrup
- 1 tbsp shredded coconut
- Pinch of salt

**Instructions:**
1. Mix chia seeds and coconut milk in jar. Stir well (chia clumps!).
2. Let sit 5 minutes, stir again.
3. Add mango, maple syrup, coconut, and salt. Stir.
4. Refrigerate overnight (or at least 4 hours).
5. Stir before eating. Top with fresh fruit if desired.

#### Savory Spiced Oatmeal Base

**Yield:** 3 servings | **Storage:** 4 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 1.5 cups rolled oats
- 3 cups vegetable broth
- 1 tbsp coconut oil
- 1/2 tsp ground turmeric
- 1/2 tsp ground cumin
- 1/4 tsp curry powder
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- 1 inch ginger, grated

**Instructions:**
1. Toast oats in dry pot over medium heat for 2 minutes.
2. Add coconut oil, all spices, and ginger. Stir 1 minute.
3. Add broth. Bring to simmer, cook 5-7 minutes until creamy.
4. Divide into 3 portions. Cool and store.

**To serve:** Reheat portion in microwave. Top with: sautéed greens, sliced avocado, crispy chickpeas, drizzle of sesame oil, green onions.

### Snacks

#### Curried Cashews

**Yield:** 1.5 cups | **Storage:** 2 weeks at room temperature

**Ingredients:**
- 1.5 cups raw cashews
- 1 tbsp coconut oil, melted
- 2 tsp curry powder
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 tsp cayenne
- 1 tsp maple syrup

**Instructions:**
1. Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C).
2. Toss cashews with all ingredients until coated.
3. Spread on lined sheet pan.
4. Bake 12-15 minutes, stirring halfway, until golden.
5. Cool completely (they'll crisp up). Store in airtight container.

---

#### Coconut Date Balls

**Yield:** 16 balls | **Storage:** 2 weeks refrigerated, 2 months frozen

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup Medjool dates, pitted (~10-12 dates)
- 1/2 cup shredded unsweetened coconut, plus more for rolling
- 1/2 cup raw cashews
- 2 tbsp natural peanut butter
- 1/4 tsp cinnamon
- Pinch of salt

**Instructions:**
1. Add all ingredients to food processor.
2. Process until mixture comes together and is sticky, 1-2 minutes.
3. Roll into 16 balls.
4. Roll in extra shredded coconut.
5. Store in container in fridge.

---

#### Crispy Chickpea Poppers

**Yield:** ~2 cups | **Storage:** 5 days at room temperature

**Ingredients:**
- 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and dried VERY well
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp onion powder
- 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/4 tsp cayenne
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1 tbsp sesame seeds

**Instructions:**
1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
2. Pat chickpeas completely dry.
3. Toss with sesame oil.
4. Spread on sheet pan, roast 35-40 minutes, shaking every 10 minutes.
5. Toss hot chickpeas with spices and sesame seeds.
6. Cool completely. Store in airtight container.
