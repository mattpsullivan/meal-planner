## Component Recipes

### Grains & Starches

#### Mujadara (Lentils & Rice with Crispy Onions)

**Yield:** ~8 cups | **Storage:** 5 days refrigerated

This Lebanese classic is pure comfort food - earthy lentils, fluffy rice, and sweet crispy onions.

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup green or brown lentils, rinsed
- 1 cup long-grain white rice, rinsed
- 4 cups vegetable broth or water
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp ground cumin
- 1/4 tsp ground cinnamon
- 1/4 tsp allspice
- 1/4 tsp black pepper

**For crispy onions:**
- 3 large yellow onions, halved and thinly sliced
- 1/3 cup olive oil
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt

**Instructions:**
1. **Crispy onions (start first - takes 30-40 min):** Heat oil in large skillet over medium heat. Add onions and salt. Cook, stirring occasionally, until deeply golden and caramelized, 30-40 minutes. Don't rush! Set aside half for topping; the other half goes into the lentils.
2. **Lentils:** In pot, combine lentils, 3 cups broth, and spices. Bring to boil, reduce to simmer, cook 15 minutes.
3. Add rice and remaining 1 cup broth. Stir, bring back to simmer, cover, cook 15-18 minutes until rice is tender.
4. Stir in half the caramelized onions.
5. Fluff and store. Top with remaining crispy onions when serving.

**Serving:** Top with fresh parsley, a dollop of yogurt, and extra crispy onions.

#### Fluffy Couscous

**Yield:** ~4 cups | **Storage:** 5 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 2 cups couscous
- 2 cups boiling water or vegetable broth
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
- Zest of 1 lemon

**Instructions:**
1. Place couscous, oil, and salt in bowl.
2. Pour boiling liquid over top. Cover tightly with plastic wrap or plate.
3. Let sit 5 minutes.
4. Fluff with fork. Stir in parsley and lemon zest.
5. Cool and store.

---

#### Creamy Mashed Potatoes

**Yield:** ~5 cups | **Storage:** 4 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 4 large russet potatoes, peeled and cubed
- 4 tbsp vegan butter
- 1/2 cup oat milk (warm)
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1/4 tsp black pepper

**Instructions:**
1. Boil potatoes in salted water until fork-tender, 15-20 minutes.
2. Drain well. Return to pot.
3. Add butter, warm milk, salt, garlic powder, and pepper.
4. Mash until smooth and creamy. Taste and adjust.
5. Store in container.

**Reheating:** Microwave with splash of milk, stir.

### Proteins

#### Baked Falafel

**Yield:** ~20 falafel | **Storage:** 5 days refrigerated, 2 months frozen

**Ingredients:**
- 2 cans (15 oz each) chickpeas, drained, rinsed, and dried well
- 1 small yellow onion, roughly chopped
- 4 cloves garlic
- 1 cup fresh parsley (packed)
- 1/2 cup fresh cilantro (packed)
- 2 tbsp fresh dill (optional)
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- 1/4 tsp cayenne
- 3 tbsp flour (all-purpose or chickpea)
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 3 tbsp olive oil (for baking)

**Instructions:**
1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Line sheet pan with parchment, brush with oil.
2. Add onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, and dill to food processor. Pulse until finely chopped.
3. Add dried chickpeas (pat them dry!). Pulse until mixture is finely ground but NOT a paste - should have texture.
4. Add cumin, coriander, salt, pepper, cayenne, flour, and baking powder. Pulse to combine.
5. Scoop 2 tablespoons mixture, form into patties (not balls - they cook more evenly flat).
6. Place on prepared pan. Brush tops with olive oil.
7. Bake 15 minutes, flip, bake another 10-12 minutes until golden and crispy.
8. Cool and store. Reheat in oven or air fryer.

#### Hearty Vegan Chili

**Yield:** ~10 cups | **Storage:** 5 days refrigerated, 3 months frozen

**Ingredients:**
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 large yellow onion, diced
- 1 bell pepper, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2-3 jalapeños, seeded and minced
- 2 tbsp chili powder
- 1 tbsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- 1/2 tsp cinnamon
- 1/4 tsp cayenne (more for heat)
- 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
- 1 can (14 oz) fire-roasted diced tomatoes
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 2 cans (15 oz each) black beans, drained and rinsed
- 2 cans (15 oz each) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 cup vegetable broth
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tbsp maple syrup
- 2 tbsp lime juice

**Instructions:**
1. **Instant Pot:** Sauté onion and pepper in oil 5 minutes. Add garlic, jalapeños, and spices, cook 1 minute. Add everything else except lime. Pressure cook 15 minutes, natural release.
2. **Stovetop:** Same start. Simmer 45 minutes to 1 hour, stirring occasionally.
3. Remove bay leaves. Stir in lime juice.
4. Taste and adjust seasoning.

**Serving:** Top with avocado, vegan sour cream, cilantro, green onions. Serve with cornbread.

#### BBQ Lentils

**Yield:** ~4 cups | **Storage:** 5 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup green or brown lentils, rinsed
- 2.5 cups water
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1/2 medium yellow onion, finely diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 cup BBQ sauce (store-bought vegan)
- 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/2 tsp onion powder
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- Salt to taste

**Instructions:**
1. Cook lentils in water until tender, 20-25 minutes. Drain excess liquid.
2. Heat oil in skillet. Sauté onion 5 minutes, add garlic, cook 1 minute.
3. Add cooked lentils, BBQ sauce, vinegar, and spices.
4. Simmer 10 minutes, stirring, until thick and saucy.
5. Taste and adjust. Cool and store.

**Use in:** Sandwiches, bowls over rice, loaded sweet potatoes

### Sauces

#### Green Tahini Sauce

**Yield:** ~1.5 cups | **Storage:** 7 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 1/2 cup tahini
- 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
- 1 cup fresh parsley (packed)
- 1/2 cup fresh cilantro (packed)
- 2 cloves garlic
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4-1/2 cup cold water

**Instructions:**
1. Blend tahini, lemon juice, herbs, garlic, and salt in blender.
2. Stream in water until desired consistency (pourable but thick).
3. Taste and adjust lemon/salt.
4. Store in jar. Shake before using.

---

#### Garlic Sauce (Toum-Style)

**Yield:** ~1 cup | **Storage:** 2 weeks refrigerated

This Lebanese garlic sauce is POTENT. A little goes a long way.

**Ingredients:**
- 1/2 cup raw cashews, soaked 15 min in boiling water, drained
- 8 cloves garlic (yes, 8!)
- 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 cup neutral oil
- 2-4 tbsp cold water

**Instructions:**
1. Blend cashews, garlic, lemon juice, and salt until smooth.
2. With blender running, slowly drizzle in oil until emulsified.
3. Add water to reach desired consistency.
4. Store in jar. Flavor mellows after a day.

---

#### Cashew Mac Sauce

**Yield:** ~3 cups | **Storage:** 5 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup raw cashews, soaked 15 min in boiling water, drained
- 1.5 cups oat milk
- 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
- 2 tbsp lemon juice
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp onion powder
- 1/4 tsp turmeric
- 1/4 tsp smoked paprika
- Pinch of cayenne

**Instructions:**
1. Blend all ingredients until completely smooth, 2 minutes.
2. Pour into saucepan, cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until thickened, 5-7 minutes.
3. Taste and adjust seasoning.
4. Store. Reheat with splash of milk, whisking.

**For Mac & Cheese:** Cook 1 lb pasta. Toss with sauce. Top with breadcrumbs and broil if desired.

### Roasted Vegetables

#### Za'atar Roasted Vegetables

**Yield:** ~6 cups | **Storage:** 5 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 1 head cauliflower, cut into florets
- 2 zucchini, cut into half-moons
- 2 bell peppers, cut into chunks
- 1 red onion, cut into wedges
- 4 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp za'atar
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper

**Instructions:**
1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
2. Toss vegetables with oil, za'atar, salt, and pepper.
3. Spread on sheet pans in single layer.
4. Roast 25-30 minutes, stirring halfway, until tender and charred.
5. Cool and store.

---

#### Roasted Broccoli

**Yield:** ~4 cups | **Storage:** 4 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 2 heads broccoli, cut into florets
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 4 cloves garlic, smashed
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
- Lemon wedges for serving

**Instructions:**
1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
2. Toss broccoli with oil, garlic, salt, and pepper flakes.
3. Spread on sheet pan.
4. Roast 20-25 minutes until charred and crispy at edges.
5. Squeeze lemon over top. Store.

### Breakfast Components

#### Shakshuka-Style Beans

**Yield:** 4 servings | **Storage:** 4 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 small onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
- 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 tsp cayenne
- Fresh parsley for garnish

**Instructions:**
1. Heat oil in skillet. Sauté onion 5 minutes.
2. Add garlic and spices, cook 1 minute.
3. Add tomatoes and chickpeas. Simmer 15 minutes until thickened.
4. Store in container.

**To serve:** Reheat portion. Serve with crusty bread or pita for scooping. Top with fresh parsley, drizzle of olive oil, and optional avocado.

#### Banana Oat Pancake Batter

**Yield:** ~8 pancakes | **Storage:** 3 days refrigerated (batter) or freeze cooked pancakes

**Ingredients:**
- 2 cups rolled oats
- 2 ripe bananas
- 1.5 cups oat milk
- 2 tbsp maple syrup
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 1/2 tsp cinnamon
- Pinch of salt
- Oil for cooking

**Instructions:**
1. Blend all ingredients (except oil) until smooth.
2. Store batter in jar in fridge.

**To cook:**
1. Heat non-stick pan over medium, lightly oil.
2. Pour 1/4 cup batter per pancake.
3. Cook until bubbles form, flip, cook until golden.
4. Serve with maple syrup, fresh fruit, nut butter.

**Tip:** Make all pancakes on prep day, store stacked with parchment between. Reheat in toaster.

### Snacks

#### Baba Ganoush

**Yield:** ~2 cups | **Storage:** 5 days refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 2 medium eggplants
- 3 tbsp tahini
- 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 2 tbsp olive oil, plus more for drizzling
- Fresh parsley for garnish
- Smoked paprika for garnish

**Instructions:**
1. Char eggplants: either directly over gas flame (turn with tongs, 10-15 min) or under broiler (turn every 5 min, 20 min total). Skin should be completely charred, flesh should be soft.
2. Let cool slightly. Scoop flesh into colander, let drain 10 minutes (removes bitterness).
3. Mash eggplant flesh with fork (or pulse briefly in food processor - don't over-blend).
4. Stir in tahini, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and olive oil.
5. Taste and adjust. Transfer to serving dish.
6. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle paprika and parsley.

**Serve with:** Pita bread, raw vegetables, crackers

---

#### Almond Butter Stuffed Dates

**Yield:** 12 dates | **Storage:** 2 weeks refrigerated

**Ingredients:**
- 12 Medjool dates, pitted
- 1/4 cup almond butter
- Flaky sea salt
- Optional: crushed almonds, shredded coconut, cacao nibs

**Instructions:**
1. Slit each date lengthwise (don't cut through).
2. Fill with 1 tsp almond butter.
3. Sprinkle with flaky salt and optional toppings.
4. Store in container.

---

#### Spiced Popcorn

**Yield:** ~10 cups | **Storage:** 3 days at room temperature

**Ingredients:**
- 1/2 cup popcorn kernels
- 3 tbsp coconut oil or neutral oil
- 2 tbsp nutritional yeast
- 1 tsp za'atar OR 1 tsp smoked paprika + 1/2 tsp cumin
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt

**Instructions:**
1. Pop corn in pot with oil, or use air popper.
2. While hot, toss with nutritional yeast and spices.
3. Store in airtight container.
